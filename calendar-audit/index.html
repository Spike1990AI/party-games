4 Critical Issues Identified

⚠

Minimal Deep Work Time

Only 2-3 explicit "block to work" entries across the entire month. Strategic thinking and focused work are being squeezed out by meetings.

⚠

Back-to-Back Meeting Stacking

Many days show 8-10 consecutive meetings with 0-15 minute gaps. No recovery or preparation time between commitments.

⚠

Late Night Work Pattern

Regular meetings scheduled until 22:00-23:00. This impacts work-life balance and next-day performance.

⚠

Double-Booked Slots

April 2nd shows both "Nissan offers" and "Dentist" at 20:00. Calendar conflicts indicate overcommitment.

⚠

No Protected Lunch Time

"Lunch" appears only twice in the entire month. Meal breaks are being sacrificed for meetings.

5 Recommendations

1

Audit & Consolidate 1:1 Meetings

7 weekly 1:1s consume 4+ hours/week. Review each: Can Dan/Daryl be bi-weekly? Can Matt/Tom share a slot? Could some become async Loom updates?

2

Create Daily Deep Work Blocks

Block 09:00-11:00 daily (before most meetings start) as non-negotiable focus time. This alone would add 10 hours/week of strategic thinking time.

3

Implement 15-Minute Buffer Rule

No back-to-back meetings. Build in transition time for notes, preparation, and mental reset. Consider 25 or 50-minute meetings instead of 30/60.

4

Establish "No Meeting" Windows

Protect Wednesday morning OR Friday afternoon as meeting-free. This creates predictable space for deep work and catch-up.

5

Lock in Daily Lunch Break

Add 12:00-13:00 (or equivalent) as a recurring blocked event. Even 30 minutes protected for meals improves afternoon energy and decision-making.
